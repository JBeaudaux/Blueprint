A smart-watch efficiently manage emergencies

The open smart-watch is designed to notify caretakers in time of emergencies for improved emergency response. Precisely, the Blue print smart-watch integrates:

  • # Alarm button: The watch is equipped with an alarm button. When pressed, an emergency message is sent to a base station, that immediately sends an SMS/email notifying relatives or care services, thus allowing for quicker emergency management.
  • # Fall detection: Many elders have experienced falls, without being able to get back on their feet and call for help. The watch thus integrates a movement patters monitoring and analysis component, able to quickly detect critical falls and immediatly notify relatives or care services (similarly to the alarm button).

Your smart-phone helping you to improve your health

Similarly to the smart-watch, the Blue print android application can detect falls and notify users and/or relatives in case of emergency. It also integrates a set of services to help monitor, manage and improve health easily and on the the long run:

  • # Early detection of heart failure: Combined, factors such as ambient temperature, physical activity and nutrition have a strong impact on one's health, and may quickly be the cause of heart failure. Those can often be avoided by simply resting a few mements before the stroke actually occur. In this context, it is important to assess risk-level in real-time, in order to notify the user and allow him to rest in time to prevent imminent strokes. Through the constant monitoring and analysis of vital parameters, the Blue print application can detect at-risk situation before they actually occur, and notify the person and its caretakers when taking action may save his life.
  • # Physical activity level estimator: by keeping a record of movement, heart activity and other metabolic parameters, the App can keep track of the daily activity level, measure their impact on health and define achievable goals for further condition improvement.

Identify determinants of health and diseases through life-logging

A wide range of environmental and lifestyle parameters are known to have a direct influence on our health. It has even been demonstrated that daily routines can provide substantial information on people’s health and its evolution, as even the slightest changes in habits may be the cause or result of changes in health conditions. Yet, their exact correlation and interaction remain an open topic, as constantly collecting fine-grained information on a wide-range of key factors in health and disease is a very complex task.

In this context, Blue print envisions to advance the state of the art in this area by developing an integrated concept able to improve our ability to perform life-logging, providing the evidence needed to further advance our understanding of the mechanisms at work in health and diseases.
